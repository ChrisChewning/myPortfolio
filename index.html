About Me

A Short Timeline

  • Forever to Thee

    I graduated The University of South Carolina with a BA in English and minor in Print Journalism. After years of being in the music scene there & living my whole life on the East Coast I was ready for a change. I thought I would go north to enroll in Temple University's MFA Creative Writing Program but decided instead to live in Austin, Texas.

  • Teaching English

    I then taught English for seven years at Canyon Vista Middle School, Austin's top-rated middle school, which leads into a top-200 nationally ranked high school. Besides teaching 6th - 8th grades, the last few years I also worked in leadership and on the tech committee, training colleagues and leading students in tech initiatives. My final year I served as department chair, where I oversaw a team of 15 teachers, our department goals and vision, and our budget.

  • Working with Immigrants & Refugees

    When Austin Community College started their community ESL program I jumped on board, teaching six levels of 'Surival English' to adults. I also helped start their 'Workplace English' program, creating a 100-plus page workbook for ACC's janitorial staff, which will be replicated throughout the 'Continuing Education' ESL department.

  • General Assembly

    In June, 2018 the time finally came to pursue my dream of becoming a software developer. I had been passionate about coding for the last three years and have been shocked at how much you can learn in 12 weeks. Full-time coding for pretty much every waking moment has taught me how to program but also how to learn and research solutions to whatever problem is thrown my way!
